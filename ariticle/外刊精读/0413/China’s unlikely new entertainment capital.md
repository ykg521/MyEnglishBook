## Zhengzhou is emerging as the Hollywood of micro-dramas

December 4th 2025

<mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-green" data-id="950e061a-17b2-4c54-939d-7a94c4309c71">Even among Westerners who have <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="48ae44fc-cf9f-4f77-9582-7a0c53e595c7">dabbled in</mark> Chinese micro-dramas</mark>, <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-pink" data-id="0dcb32e6-e553-423b-b5e5-f284013886dd">few</mark>
will have heard of Zhengzhou, <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-green" data-id="6acc5c8f-bbe8-44ea-92c9-4fce68a67cba">the sooty city at the heart of the industry</mark>. <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-green" data-id="e76564fe-cc6b-444c-8fd1-cee50a253bde">In
recent years</mark> <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-pink" data-id="1b6c1738-0848-4e3c-8184-1ce7a9579ed6">the drab inland manufacturing hub of 13m</mark> has mostly made the
news <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-green" data-id="19083f52-5872-4db9-bf51-27b7dbd86973">for deadly floods, factory protests and the severity of its real-estate
crisis</mark>. Yet it is now churning out shows <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="4c37431e-88c2-40a4-a6d4-54051b8df32f">that are being watched around the world</mark>.

In 2023 Chinese marketing executives began shooting series of 90 episodes,
<mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="44bf4d51-4e18-4bc4-a218-5ab1e2902df9">each lasting two minutes</mark>, <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-green" data-id="581dd673-307f-4038-b5da-d57734b6cfd6">to promote the country's online-novel industry</mark>.<mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-green" data-id="a4086d06-d1f1-4333-ac96-3760d40f260d">As
social media began promoting the clips</mark>, the <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="19c5216a-6082-4f8b-94ee-094fd374384d">format</mark> <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-green" data-id="3670caf2-1282-4c2e-90e6-aa16c88c7d30">took off</mark>. Zhengzhou
<mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="7757961a-5cf1-4db2-a705-8f3eabb63b66">wisely</mark> <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-green" data-id="0b8d416e-8b13-4dd2-9246-6b1ccf883983">got in on</mark> the action early. Officials wrote micro-dramas <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-green" data-id="41096cc4-2694-4ee7-86bd-a5aa387a2de8">into economic-planning documents</mark>, built studios and established state-owned
investment funds <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-green" data-id="ef604c4c-f926-48cb-91a0-d0ce90d0fd23">dedicated to supporting the industry</mark>.

Zhengzhou's property crisis has been an asset <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="66e181a1-421d-4f7f-9172-ac66d14fcb26">to micro-drama producers</mark>.
Real-estate costs are so low <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-green" data-id="41f1115b-30a0-4fac-a963-be1ab5c86825">in the city</mark> <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="e0848049-5d4f-4620-9176-fcacb8577b09">that vast film-production complexes
can be set up on the cheap</mark>. Jumei Film Base, one of the largest studios, was
built in 2023 in a <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="02bbe786-0745-49a1-afa5-1f0b01245497">derelict</mark> shopping centre on the <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="30036100-c44c-42e3-8968-8ca5525549d4">outskirts</mark> of the city. The
industry <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-pink" data-id="0979341b-329b-4661-8398-ce0ae3dd7b2a">operates</mark> <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-green" data-id="e1d6597f-1c01-4f36-92b4-b363d8faac2c">on bare-bones budgets</mark>, <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-green" data-id="d4815edf-a124-45d6-bde1-8d9b3ebc1374">making Zhengzhou more attractive
than Beijing or Shanghai</mark>. Local actors are paid just 1,000 yuan ($141) a day
and often work <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="af5ede9c-af9e-460c-bfdd-165c719feca8">gruelling</mark> hours. Their money goes much further in China's interior.

Chinese micro-dramas are also starting to take off in the West. Some are
even produced in America, <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-green" data-id="d6a13fc4-57d8-4242-945a-070608e9ef3b">with scripts translated into English and then shot
in places such as California</mark>. But many micro-dramas aimed at Westerners
are also being produced in Zhengzhou. Foreign actors who fly into the city
can earn around $1,000 a day.

Creating content for foreign audiences on Chinese soil is <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-green" data-id="5a30a56b-f78a-40ab-b48a-64e90e316f43">sensitive</mark>. To sell
well overseas, micro-dramas need <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="6debd0ea-6f29-4ad4-90a1-76910e4c7510">racy</mark> <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-green" data-id="d727f8a9-e2f0-440b-9a3e-35ec29e59e6a">plot lines</mark> that may break the Chinese
government's strict censorship rules. As a result, <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="f29f3805-05d4-4eca-ae19-b3d937d6922e">steamy</mark> scenes with foreign
actors must be shot <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="6c599daa-7b52-4992-ad3c-0744eb69f9ff">discreetly</mark>. Your <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="43bb3bba-8bab-4d06-ba36-9cf58a60d7c7">correspondent</mark> was given freedom to
wander Jumei's facilities. But when he <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="6ea8a7ee-6ce6-4d4f-9fc9-3bfe5d689cc8">trod</mark> onto a set with <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="bbccb381-07a4-4d81-9d97-8be76a3b7ea9">scantily</mark> <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="52276cb8-bbac-4e76-916d-470e5cca7dce">clad</mark>
foreign actors, he was quickly shooed away.
