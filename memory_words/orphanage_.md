# orphanage - Comprehensive Language Analysis

## Basic Information

**Word:** orphanage
**Part of Speech:** Noun
**Pronunciation:** /ˈɔːr.fən.ɪdʒ/ (OR-fuh-nij)

## Synonyms (同义词)

- **orphan asylum** - historical term for an institution for orphans
- **children's home** - modern term for a residential care facility
- **foster home** - private home providing care for orphans
- **group home** - facility housing multiple children in care
- **institution** - general term for an organized establishment

## Associative Words (联想词)

- **orphan** - child without parents
- **adoption** - legal process of taking another's child as one's own
- **foster care** - system of caring for children in need
- **charity** - voluntary giving to those in need
- **welfare** - state of being well, especially in terms of financial and physical health
- **guardian** - person legally responsible for the care of a child
- **abandonment** - act of leaving a child without care

## Collocations (地道表达)

- **run an orphanage** - manage an orphanage
- **live in an orphanage** - reside at an orphanage
- **visit an orphanage** - go to an orphanage to see the children
- **donate to an orphanage** - give money or goods to support an orphanage
- **work at an orphanage** - be employed at an orphanage
- **close an orphanage** - shut down an orphanage facility
- **build an orphanage** - construct a new orphanage

## Common Phrases and Expressions

- **"She grew up in an orphanage after her parents passed away."**
- **"The charity organization funds several orphanages across the country."**
- **"He volunteered at the local orphanage during his summer break."**
- **"The old orphanage was converted into a community center."**

## Homophones and Easily Confused Words (发音、拼写易混词)

- **orphans** - plural of orphan (different meaning)
- **orphaned** - past tense of orphan (verb form)
- **office** - completely different word, but similar initial sound
- **orchard** - different word, but shares the "or-" beginning

## Word Forms and Inflections (变形)

- **Base form:** orphanage
- **Plural:** orphanages
- **Related adjective:** orphaned (used to describe orphans)
- **Related verb:** orphan (rare usage, meaning to make someone an orphan)

## Usage Notes

1. **Historical context:** The term "orphanage" was more commonly used in the 19th and early 20th centuries. Modern terminology often uses "children's home" or "group home."
2. **Cultural references:** The word often appears in literature and films depicting historical settings, such as Charles Dickens' works.
3. **Modern usage:** While still understood, "orphanage" has somewhat negative connotations associated with institutional care. Contemporary systems favor foster care and smaller group homes.

## Example Sentences

2. "Many historical novels feature protagonists who grew up in orphanages."
