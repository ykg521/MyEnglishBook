plural squires [count]
1: a young man in the Middle Ages who helped a knight before eventually becoming a knight himself（中世纪骑士的）扈从
2: a man in the past in England who owned most of the land in a village or district in the country（旧时英格兰的）乡绅，大地主
3: British, informal + old-fashioned — used to address a man whose name is unknown先生（对不知名字的男子的称呼）“
 “Where to, squire?” asked the taxi driver.
“去哪儿啊，先生？”出租车司机问道。
