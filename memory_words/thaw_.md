# thaw - Comprehensive Language Learning Document

## 1. Basic Information

**Word:** thaw
**Part of speech:** verb, noun
**Pronunciation:** /θɔː/ (UK), /θɑː/ (US)
**Definition:**

- *verb:* to (cause something to) change from a solid to a liquid state because of an increase in temperature
- *noun:* a period of warmer weather when ice and snow melt

## 2. Synonyms (同义词)

### Verb Synonyms:

- melt
- unfreeze
- defrost
- dissolve
- warm up
- loosen up

### Noun Synonyms:

- melt
- defrosting
- melting
- warm spell
- spring (in the context of weather)

## 3. Collocations and Common Phrases (联想词)

### Verb Collocations:

- **thaw completely** - 完全融化
- **thaw gradually** - 逐渐融化
- **thaw out** - 解冻（尤指食物）
- **thaw over time** - 随时间推移而解冻
- **thaw in the sun** - 在阳光下融化

### Noun Collocations:

- **a sudden thaw** - 突然的解冻
- **the spring thaw** - 春天的融雪
- **a thaw in relations** - 关系的缓和
- **record thaw** - 创纪录的解冻
- **early thaw** - 早来的解冻

## 4. Idiomatic Expressions and Colloquial Usage (地道表达)

### Literal Meanings:

- **Thaw the meat** - 解冻肉类
- **Thaw in the microwave** - 用微波炉解冻
- **Thaw overnight in the fridge** - 在冰箱里过夜解冻
- **The ice is thawing** - 冰正在融化

### Figurative Meanings:

- **Thaw in diplomatic relations** - 外交关系缓和
- **Thaw a cold relationship** - 缓和冷淡的关系
- **Thaw someone's heart** - 温暖某人的心
- **Break the thaw** - 打破僵局（关系）

## 5. Pronunciation and Spelling Confusion (发音、拼写易混词)

### Homophones/Homonyms:

- **saw** (过去式 of see) - 不同拼写相同发音
- **paw** - 不同拼写相同发音

### Common Confusions:

- **thaw vs. thawed** - 时态混淆
- **thaw vs. through** - 发音相似但拼写不同
- **thaw vs. though** - 发音相似但拼写不同

### Pronunciation Tips:

- Starts with "th" sound /θ/ (voiceless)
- Rhymes with "saw" and "law"
- Stress is on the first syllable

## 6. Word Forms and Inflections (变形)

### Verb Forms:

- **Base form:** thaw
- **Third person singular:** thaws
- **Present participle:** thawing
- **Past simple:** thawed
- **Past participle:** thawed

### Noun Form:

- **Singular:** thaw
- **Plural:** thaws

### Example Sentences for Each Form:

- **Present:** The ice cream begins to **thaw** in the sun.
- **Third person:** The warm weather **thaws** the frozen lake.
- **Present participle:** **Thawing** frozen food takes time.
- **Past simple:** The snow **thawed** quickly yesterday.
- **Past participle:** The meat has **thawed** completely.
- **Noun:** We had an early **thaw** this year.

## 7. Usage Notes

### Formal vs. Informal:

- **Formal:** The treaty led to a thaw in international relations.
- **Informal:** Let's thaw out some frozen pizza for dinner.

### British vs. American English:

- **British:** "Defrost" is more commonly used for food
- **American:** "Thaw" is more commonly used for all contexts

## 9. Common Mistakes to Avoid

### Grammar Mistakes:

- Incorrect: *The ice is thaw*
- Correct: *The ice is thawing* or *The ice has thawed*

### Collocation Mistakes:

- Incorrect: *thaw the water* (water doesn't need thawing)
- Correct: *thaw the ice* or *melt the ice*
