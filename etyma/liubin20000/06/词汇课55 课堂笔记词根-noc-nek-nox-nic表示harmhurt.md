## 词根-noc, nek-, nox-, nic-表示 harm, hurt, 含义为“伤害；损害；致死”。

Proto-Indo-European root meaning "death."

It is the hypothetical source of/evidence for its existence is provided by: Sanskrit nasyati "disappears, perishes," Avestan nasyeiti "disappears," nasu-"corpse," Old Persian vi-nathayatiy "he injures;" Greek nekros "corpse;" Latin nex, genitive necis "violent death, murder" (as opposed to mors), nocere "to harm, hurt," noxius "harmful;" Greek nekus "dead" (adj.), nekros "dead body, corpse;" Old Irish ec, Breton ankou, Welsh angeu "death."

innocent ['ɪnəs(ə)nt] adj. 清白的；天真的；无辜受害的；无恶意的 n. 阅历浅的人；天真的人 2799

词源：mid-14c., "doing no evil; free from sin, guilt, or moral wrong," from Old French inocent "harmless; not guilty; pure" (12c.), from Latin innocentem (nominative innocens) "not guilty, blameless; harmless; disinterested," from in- "not" (see in- (1)) + nocentem (nominative nocens), present participle of nocere "to harm," from *nok-s-, suffixed form of PIE root *nek- (1) "death."
助记：in-(=not：不；非)+ -noc(=to harm：伤害)+ -ent(形容词后缀)→ 即没有伤害的 → 引申词义清白的；无恶意的；天真的人
搭配：an innocent question：没有恶意的问题  
innocent victims：无辜的受害者  
innocent of any crime：清白无罪的

词源：来自拉丁语 nectar，来自希腊语 nektar，据说是古希腊众神饮用的琼浆玉液。由 nek-(=death：死亡)和-tar(=overcome：战胜)组成，字面意思为“战胜死亡”。据说神仙们饮用后可以永葆美貌并长生不老。引申义为花蜜，果汁饮料。

noxious ['nɒkʃəs] adj. 有毒的；有害的；令人厌恶的

15912

词源: Unwholesome, harmful. 来自拉丁语 noxa(injury, hurt: 损害; 伤害) → noxius (hurtful, injurious: 伤害的; 有害的) → noxious (有毒的; 有害的)。From PIE *noks-, from root *nek-"death." Related: Noxiously; noxiousness.
助记：nox(=-noc-=to hurt:伤害)+-ious(形容词后缀，...的)→造成伤害的→有毒的；有害的
搭配：noxious fumes：有毒烟雾  
noxious gas：有害气体

obnoxious [əb'nɒkfəs] adj. 令人讨厌的；可憎的 14303
助记：ob- (= to) + -nox (=injury, hurt: 伤害；受伤) + -ious (形容词后缀) → 引起伤害的 → 引申词义令人讨厌的，可憎的
搭配：obnoxious behavior：讨厌的行为

ob-的常见用法：

1、表示 to / toward

2、表示 “against：相反”；例如：abnormal

pernicious [pəˈnɪʃəs] adj. 极为有害的；恶性的(尤指潜移默化地) 16590

词源：Early 15c., from Middle French pernicios (13c., Modern French pernicieux) and directly from Latin perniciosus "destructive," from pernicies "destruction, death, ruin". Related to necare "to kill," nocere "to hurt, injure, harm," noxa "harm, injury". Related: Perniciously; perniciousness.
助记：per- (=completely: 完全地) + -nic- (=harm, injury: 伤害；损害) + -ious (形容词后缀)→ 彻底有损害的 → 极为有害的；恶性的
搭配：the pernicious effects of poverty：贫穷的危害性  

the media’s pernicious influence：媒体的有害影响  

pernicious malaria：热带疟；凶险型疟疾

nuisance['njuːs(ə)ns] n. 麻烦事；讨厌的人(或东西)；妨害行为 8901

词源：来自拉丁语 nocere = to hurt: 伤害 → 古法语 nuisance = harm, wrong, damage: 伤害；犯罪；损害 → 后词义弱化，指麻烦事，讨厌的人或东西，妨害行为。
搭配：make a nuisance of oneself: 惹人讨厌

be a nuisance: 讨人嫌

noise nuisance: 噪声危害

## Pierce 表示 “刺穿；穿孔；穿过”

pierce [pɪəs] vt. 刺穿，刺破；穿孔，穿洞；(声或光)穿过，透入 6949

词源：late 13c. "make a hole in; force one's way through," from Anglo-French perser, Old French percier "pierce, transfix, drive through" (12c., Modern French percer), probably from Vulgar Latin *pertusiare, frequentative of Latin pertusus, past participle of pertundere "to thrust or bore through," from per "through" (from PIE root *per- (1) "forward," hence "through") + tundere "to beat, pound," from PIE *tund-, from root *(s)teu-"to push, strike, knock, beat, thrust" (see obtuse). Related: Pierced; piercing.
助记：来自古法语 percer，穿透，刺穿，改写自拉丁语 pertundere，穿透，刺穿。

来自 per-(通过；穿过)+ tundere(击；打；捣)→ 穿透；刺穿
搭配：a bullet pierce her lung：子弹射穿肺部

have your ears/nose etc. pierced：在耳朵和鼻子上扎洞

pierce the silence：划破了寂静

pierce through the dense mist：穿透了浓雾

## 单词 frame 及在其所构成的单词中表示 “框；框架”。

frame [freɪm] n. 架；框架；构架；支架；骨架；(电影的)画面，镜头，帧； vt. 给(图画或照片)配框；制定(规则、计划或体系)；诬陷 1848
助记：fram(=border：边框，边界)→边框，框架 →用来指建筑框架和基础结构 →后词义通用化为构架；环绕；表达；制定规则；木制的
搭配：time frame：时间表，时间范围
modern timber frames：现代化的木制构架
bony frames：瘦骨嶙峋的躯体
in the frame for something：在…考虑范围内；有机会成为…
frame an innocent person：诬陷一个无辜的人
projects at 16 frames per second：每秒放映16帧画面
be framed by tropical gardens：四周环绕着热带花园
a white-painted frame house：一个漆成白色的木制房子
例句：During the same time frame, the University of Northern Iowa graduated 67% of its white students, but only 39% of its blacks.
在相同的时间段内，北爱荷华大学白人学生毕业率为 67%，而黑人学生毕业率只有 39%。
仿写例句：在相同的时间段内，人们阅读流行杂志的时间要远远多余经典书籍。
During the same time frame, people spend more time in reading popular journals than classics.

## far-, further 表示遥远的；更远的等含义。其来源于词根“far-; fur-; ford-”，也来源于词根“per-”，表示“farther, remote, ulterior, to a great distance, beyond”=“更远的；遥远的；将来的；远距离的；更远处”的含义。

far [faː] adv. 很远地；(距离)远至；久远地；到…程度；(进展)到…程度；(人或行为)极端到…程度；adj. 较远一端的；(离中心)最大距离的 253
词源：Middle English fer, from Old English feorr "far, remote, distant," from Proto-Germanic *ferera- (cognates: Old Saxon fer, Old Frisian fer, Old Norse fjarre, Dutch ver, Old High German ferro, German fern), probably a development in western Proto-Germanic from the adverb (see far (adv.)). Far East "China, Japan, and surrounding regions" is from 1838.

afar [ə'fɑː] adv. 在远处；从远处 13543
助记：a-(在复合词中含义为 of 或 on)+ far(遥远的)→ 在远处；从远处
搭配：see from afar：从远处看
love someone from afar：暗恋
表达“暗恋”最地道和常用的英文说法主要有以下几种：
Have a crush on someone: 这是最口语化、最常用的表达，尤其在年轻人中。它通常指一种短暂而强烈的迷恋或爱慕之情。
Example: I've had a crush on my classmate for months. (我暗恋我的同学好几个月了。)
Be in love with someone (from afar): 这个表达更深刻，指深爱着某人，而“from afar”（从远处）明确点出了“暗恋”的距离感和不敢表白的状态。
Example: She's been in love with him from afar since middle school. (她从中学开始就一直暗恋他。)
Have a thing for someone: 这是一种非正式的说法，表示对某人有特别的感觉或吸引力。
Example: He has a thing for his coworker. (他暗恋他的同事。)
Have feelings for someone: 这是一个比较委婉和通用的表达，指对某人有爱慕之情，但不明确是否是单方面的暗恋。
Example: I have strong feelings for her, but I don't know if she feels the same way. (我对她有很深的感情，但我不知道她是否也有同感。)
afar off：远离着

far-fetched ['faːˈfetʃt] 穿凿附会的；不切实际的；牵强的 15646
