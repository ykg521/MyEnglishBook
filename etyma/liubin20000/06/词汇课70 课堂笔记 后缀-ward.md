## 有时词根“ward”也表示“方向；朝向”

词源：adverbial suffix expressing direction, Old English -weard "toward," literally "turned toward," sometimes -weardes, with genitive singular ending of neuter adjectives, from Proto-Germanic *werda- (cognates: Old Saxon, Old Frisian -ward, Old Norse -verðr), variant of PIE *werto- "to turn, wind," from root *wer- (2) "to turn, bend." The original notion is of "turned toward."

awkward ['ɔːkwəd] adj. 令人尴尬的；(使用)不便的；棘手的 (工作)；笨拙的；难为情的 5053

词源：Mid-14c. (adv.), "in the wrong direction". As an adjective, "turned the wrong way," 1510s. Meaning "clumsy, wanting ease and grace in movement" recorded by 1520s. Of persons, the meaning "embarrassed, ill-at-ease" is from 1713s. Related: Awkwardly.
助记：awk(=back：背的，背后的)+ ward(表方向性：向)→ 向后面的，向背后的 → 不顺手的(即：用手不好处理的)，难操纵的；笨拙的，不灵活的 → 引申义为令人尴尬的；棘手的；难为情的

搭配：an awkward gesture：一个笨拙的手势

feel awkward: 感觉到别扭，尴尬

awkward questions: 尴尬的问题

make it awkward to carry: 不便携带

例句：If you feel awkward being humorous, you must practice so that it becomes more natural.

如果你在幽默的时候感到尴尬，你必须练习一下以至于能够更自然一些。
