单词 nice, science, conscience, prescient 都来自词根 sci-，它表示“to know, to separate one thing from another, to distinguish”，即含义为“知道，将一物与其他分开，辨别；区分，使有别于”。来自古词根 skei-，“to cut, split”含义为“切割，分开，分裂”。

science ['saɪəns] n. 科学；自然科学；学科；(人类行为的)科学研究 892

词源：14 世纪中期，“what is known, knowledge (of something) acquired by study; information;” “已知之物，从研究学习中习得的知识；信息”。也有 "assurance of knowledge, certitude, certainty", “对知识的确定；确实的事；必然的事”。来自古法语 science "knowledge, learning, application; corpus of human knowledge" (12c.), 来自拉丁语 scientia "knowledge, a knowing; expertness," 来自 sciens (genitive scientis) "intelligent, skilled," scire "to know" 的现在分词形式，也许来自 "to separate one thing from another, to distinguish," 与 scindere "to cut, divide" 相关，来自词根 *skeɪ- "to cut, split".
助记：sci-(= to know：知道)+ -ence(名词后缀)→ 帮助人类了解、“知道”周围的世界的事物 → 科学；自然科学；学科；科学研究

搭配：science and technology：科学与技术

computer science：计算机科学

the modern science of psychology：现代心理学

## 词根-tract 表示“拉”，与单词“draw：拉，拽，抽”，此外，词根“-treat”也表示“拖；拉”的含义。

词源："area," mid-15c., "period or lapse of time," from Latin tractus "track, course, space, duration," lit, "a drawing out or pulling," from stem of trahere "to pull, draw," from PIE root *tragh-"to draw, drag, move".

drag [dræɡ] vt. (使劲而吃力地)拖，拉，拽，扯；缓慢而费力地移动(或行进)n. 令人厌烦的人；拖累，绊脚石 2146

词源：Late 15c., "to draw away by force, pull haul", 含义为“用力拉开，拖，拉”。from Old Norse draga, or a dialectal variant of Old English dragan "to draw," both from Proto-Germanic *draganan "to draw, pull," perhaps from a PIE *dhregh- "to draw, drag on the ground".
助记：drag=draw by force, pull, haul→使劲而吃力地拖，拉；缓慢而费力地移动(或行进)

搭配：drag the chair over to the window：把椅子拖到了窗口那边

例句：Walking’s a drag—let’s drive there. 步行太累了——咱们开车去吧

Having to work late every day is a drag. 每天都得晚下班可真讨厌。
