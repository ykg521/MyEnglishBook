terrible['terəbl] adj. 可怕的；糟糕的；极其严重的

1958

词源：late 14c., "causing terror, awe, or dread; frightful," from Old French terrible (12c.), from Latin terribilis "frightful," from terrere "fill with fear," from PIE root *tros- "to make afraid" (source also of Sanskrit trasanti "to tremble, be afraid," Avestan tarshta "scared, afraid," Greek treein "to tremble, be afraid," Lithuanian trišeti "to tremble, shiver," Old Church Slavonic treso "I shake," Middle Irish tarrach "timid"). Weakened sense of "very bad, awful" is first attested 1590s.
助记：terr(= -trem = tremble：颤抖)+ible(能...的)→能让人颤抖的→可怕的；糟糕的；极其严重的

搭配：a terrible nightmare：一个可怕的噩梦

## 单词 sheath、schizophrenia、schism 都与 split、“分裂”有关。

sheath [ʃiːθ] n.(刀、剑等的)鞘；护套

词源：来自词根 skei- "to cut, split," a split stick with the sword blade inserted：刀、剑的刀片插入两片分开的木片之中，即刀、剑等的鞘中
助记：sheath=scabbard：(刀、剑的)鞘

搭配：the sheath around an electric cable：电线护皮

schizophrenia [,skɪtsə(ʊ)ˈfriːnɪə] n. 精神分裂症 12243

词源：1912，来自现代拉丁语，字面含义为"a splitting of the mind," 来自希腊语 skhizein "to split", 含义为“分裂”。
助记：schizo-(=to split：分裂)+ -phren(=heart, mind：心灵；想法)+ -ia(表疾病)→ 引申词义精神分裂症

释义：(N-UNCOUNT)精神分裂症 Schizophrenia is a serious mental illness. People who suffer from it are unable to relate their thoughts and feelings to what is happening around them and often withdraw from society.

-ia 名词后缀，表示某种疾病：

insomnia 失眠症(in-不+somn-睡眠+-ia)

myopia 近视(my- = mute：关闭；减弱 + op- 眼睛+-ia)

schism ['sɪz(ə)m;'skɪz(ə)m] n. 分裂；宗派活动；(尤指)教会分裂 18760

词源：来自14世纪晚期单词scisme, "dissension within the church", 教会派系纷争。来自古法语scisme, cisme "a cleft, split" (12世纪)，来自希腊语skhisma "division, cleft"，含义为“分裂；裂缝”。来自词根skhizein "to split"，分裂(成不同的派别)。原指欧洲的基督教教会分裂活动，后词义通用化。

例句：The church seems to be on the brink of schism. 该教会看来处于分裂的边缘。
