former ['fɔːmə] adj. 前任的；(国家) 已不存在的；疆界已变的；昔日的 pron. 前者 n. 形成者；样板 443

词源："earlier in time," mid-12c., comparative of forme "first, earliest in time or order," from Old English forma "first," from Proto-Germanic *fruma-, *furma-, from PIE *pre-mo-, suffixed (superlative) form of root *per- (1) "forward," hence "in front of, before, first." Probably patterned on formest (see foremost); it is an unusual case of a comparative formed from a superlative (the Old English -m is a superlative suffix). As "first of two," 1580s.

词源：来自古英语 forma 的比较级，来自 PIE*pre-mo，最早的*pre-，在先，词源同 far，*mo，最高级后缀，词源同 intimate，ultimate. 该词实际上是最高级的比较级。比较 foremost.
助记：form (= pre-：在先的) + -er(形容词比较级)→ 前任的；前者

搭配：former president Richard Nixon：前总统理查德·尼克松  
the former Soviet Union：前苏联  
the former home of Robert E. Lee：罗伯特·E·李的故居

forefather ['fɔːfəːðə] n. 祖先；祖宗(尤指男性)

19577

词源："ancestor," c. 1300, from fore- + father (n.); perhaps modeled on or modified from Old Norse forfaðir. Similar formation in Dutch voorvader, German Vorvater, Danish forfædre (Old English had forð-fæder).
助记：fore- (before in time: 现在之前的时间) + father(父辈)→ 祖先；祖宗(尤指男性)

before [bɪˈfɔː] conj. 在…以前；在…发生之前，以防；…之后才 prep.(时间上)在…以前；(名单或次序上)较…为先，在…之前；在…面前，当着…的面 adv. 先前，从前；(the day/week/month etc)before：前一天/一个星期/一个月等；在前面 220

词源：来源于日耳曼语 biforana，在古英语中是 beforan，后半部 foran 同时是现代英语中 for 的词源，最初的意义是 “先前”，逐步发展为现代的意义。
助记：be-(=by：经由)+ fore-(=foran：前面)组成 → 在...以前；较...为先；从前

搭配：the day before yesterday：前天  
the task before us：面临的任务

beforehand [bɪˈfɔːhænd] adv. 事先；预先；提前 10747

词源：also before-hand, "in anticipation," early 13c., from before + hand, which here is of uncertain signification, unless the original notion is payment in advance or something done before another's hand does it. Hyphenated from 18c.; one word from 19c.
助记：before(在…之前)+ hand(手)→ 在另一个人的手之前先做 → 引申义为事先；预先；提前

搭配：feel nervous beforehand：在…之前感到紧张

pristine ['pristi:n; -stain] adj. 极其新鲜的；非常洁净的；崭新的，簇新的；未受损害的；处于原始状态的 8395

词源：1530s, "pertaining to the earliest period, primitive, ancient," from Middle French pristin or directly from Latin pristinus "former, early, original". Meaning "unspoiled, untouched, pure" is from 1899 (implied in a use of pristinely) but according to OED 2nd ed. print still regarded as ignorant "by many educated speakers." 直接来自拉丁语 pristinus=former, early, original: 先前的；早期的；原始的。词根来自古拉丁语 pri- (=before: 先前；早先) + stine → unspoiled, untouched, pure: 未受污染的；纯净的 → 引申词义十分干净的；清新的；原始状态的
